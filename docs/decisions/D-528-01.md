## Context

D-526-01 closed #526 by confirming serial as the permanent posture: it refuted the walkthrough-internal
N-worker dispatcher (C3) and, among alternatives considered, labelled cross-chain parallelism (C1)
"SAFE but FUTILE — Floor = max(t_chain) = unchanged ~574s claude chain; collapsing codex/gitlab/gitea
into claude's shadow gains nothing on the bottleneck" (D-526-01:146-149). That futility verdict was
stated for the SINGLE claude chain.

#528 reopens C1 on a frame D-526-01 did not measure: the **four-chain `npm test` gate**
(`claude && codex && gitlab && gitea`), where serial cost is Σ t_chain ≈ 25-28 min and the concurrent
ideal is max(t_chain) ≈ t_claude ≈ ~10 min — a theoretical win of ≈ 3× t_claude, far from futile IF the
four chains do not contend on a constrained host. The C3 killers do not auto-carry: granularity is
whole-chain (four coarse legs, each with its own exit code) rather than intra-chain; the makespan model
is Σ→max (shadow-collapse of three chains) rather than single-chain Amdahl; and the #307 propagation
surface is the four-copy `run-chains.js` dispatch loop only, not the six-copy walkthrough family.

This run was a **#486 Case-B read-only shaping investigation** (probe → assume → adversarially falsify
→ converge). It executed real timed experiments and an exhaustive structural attribution test, then
adversarially attempted to refute the ship-it claim under the inverted burden of precedence #1
(accuracy non-negotiable; a flaky correctness gate is the most expensive outcome).

## Question

On the four-chain `npm test` frame (not the single claude chain D-526-01 measured), does bounded
concurrent dispatch in `run-chains.js` yield a median four-chain makespan win decisively exceeding 2× the
serial stddev of the TOTAL four-chain duration on a contended ≤4-core host, while preserving deterministic
first-failure attribution and canonical receipt ordering — i.e. is it worth shipping?

Per precedence #1 the burden is on parallelism: the affirmative ship-it claim wins only if it can be
established on a HOST-VALID basis. An 18-core number is an upper bound that does not transfer to a
≤4-core target.

## Decision

**The affirmative ship-it claim is NOT FINALIZABLE on the available 18-core evidence. Serial four-chain
`npm test` stays. No code change ships.**

The four-chain frame is genuinely NOT futile the way single-chain C1 was — the ideal-core win is real and
attribution is structurally clean (see Evidence) — but the win's EXISTENCE is gated on un-measurable
≤4-core contention behavior. Under the inverted burden, an un-settleable affirmative win-claim is refuted,
not "probably fine." This is a docs-only record with zero code blast radius; no #307 obligation attaches.

## Evidence and measurements

**Host caveat (lead):** All measurements ran on an 18-core host (`hw.ncpu=18`, `hw.physicalcpu=18`), node
v24.14.0, 128 GB, arm64. AC#3 and D-526-01 reopen condition #2 require a contended ≤4-core host for a
VALID make-or-break number. macOS has NO hard core-cap (no `taskset`, no cgroups, no cpuset;
`taskpolicy` is a QoS hint, not a cap; `cpulimit` is coarse SIGSTOP/SIGCONT and may not cap spawned
child trees; background load is uncontrolled; Docker-on-macOS runs a Linux VM). Every favorable number
below therefore carries the 18-core upper-bound caveat and does not transfer.

**A-KILL-1 / A-KILL-2 — make-or-break makespan + contention knee (the DECISIVE refutation).**
A synthetic chain-tree proxy (each "chain" = a node process serially spawning inner child trees of
CPU-hash + git-like fs churn), 7 interleaved warm reps, rep1 discarded:

- 18-core, no contention: serial median **1335ms** (stddev 14.4ms), N=2 **734ms**, N=4 **429ms**.
  The N=4 win (~906ms) ≫ the proxy 2σ bar (~28.8ms) — but the proxy's ~1% stddev is a deterministic-proxy
  artifact, NOT the real walkthrough's ~26% jitter (D-523-01:43 / D-526-01:73), so the margin does NOT
  transfer (the forbidden non-transferable upper bound).
- 16 background spinners (attempted ≤4-core emulation): N=4 still **510ms** vs serial **1820ms** — STILL
  wins. Empirically confirms macOS background load does NOT reproduce a hard core cap.
- CPU-heavy oversubscription sweep: at M/cores = 4.0 (M=72), concurrent = 0.10× serial — NEVER inverts.
  The proxy's tree-serialized inner work degrades gracefully and CANNOT reproduce D-526-01 Exp2b inversion
  (N=36/18c → ~2× slower). The ≤4-core inversion regime is un-reproducible here.
- Realistic four-chain bar (26% per-chain jitter, independent-sum): the IDEAL (∞-core) win clears the
  2σ bar — **light 460s vs 232s; heavy 860s vs 395s** — but only under "IF cores suffice." On ≤4 cores,
  four chains each saturating the box cannot reach the max-of-chains floor; the achievable win is bounded
  by ~Σ_work/4 and MAY NOT clear the 232-395s bar. That premise is exactly what cannot be measured here.

→ The affirmative win cannot be established; the only directionally-valid result (18-core best case
"wins") is the non-transferable upper bound the host-validity rule forbids as a finalizer.

**A-KILL-3 — attribution / canonical ordering (settled on any host; NOT a blocker).**
5000 trials across all 24 completion orderings through the proposed `KNOWN_CHAINS.indexOf` re-sort:
**0 mismatches** — deterministic canonical receipt order, deterministic first-failing-chain report,
deterministic failed-list and exit. All three real receipt consumers are SET-WISE
(`plan-validator.js:2552-2557` `filter`, `release.js:258-262` `for-of`, `gap-sweep.js:100-106` `filter`);
none reads `chains[0]`, so the gate is order-independent even WITHOUT the re-sort. Whole-chain granularity
genuinely AVOIDS C3's Exp4 misattribution (which mis-reported the failing unit 4/5 reps under concurrency,
D-526-01:90-94). This is the structural way C1 positively differs from the refuted C3 — and it is NOT a
reason to reject A.

**A-KILL-4 — cross-chain race (no hazard OBSERVED).**
claude vs codex `mkdtemp` prefix sets (199 vs 44) are exact-string disjoint; gitlab/gitea are
`kw-gl-`/`kw-gt-` namespaced; `mkdtempSync` adds an OS-random suffix. `run-chains.js` writes exactly ONE
shared file (the receipt) ONCE post-loop — not per-chain — so concurrency cannot clobber it; ZERO
`~/.config` writes on the chain path. 5 concurrent fs-race runs: 0 failures, 0 leaked tmp dirs.
**Caveat (D-526-01:96-101): a clean proxy is NOT positive proof** of race-freedom in the real
walkthroughs — it only fails to falsify n1's race-safety preconditions (a)(b)(c).

**A-KILL-5 — #307 propagation.** Not exercised (no build on fork B). The four-copy `run-chains.js`
dispatch-loop-only change is the cheapest cross-edition surface but stays unbuilt and unverified.

**Decision-bar arithmetic (honest).** The proxy serial stddev ~14.4ms (~1%) → 2σ bar ~28.8ms, cleared by
~30× — but that ~1% is a proxy artifact, not the real ~26% walkthrough jitter, so the margin does not
transfer. The realistic four-chain 2σ bar is ≈ 232s (light) / 395s (heavy); the ideal-core win clears it,
the ≤4-core achievable win (~Σ_work/4) is unmeasurable here.

## Consequences

- **No code change ships.** Docs-only record, zero code blast radius; no #307 obligation attaches to
  closing #528.
- **Serial four-chain `npm test` stays the posture.** The existing **selective-execution** lever
  (`run-chains.js` `requestedChains` path — `--chains <list>` / `--only`, `run-chains.js:208`) remains the
  zero-concurrency-risk way to cut makespan: a contributor who touched only some editions runs only those
  chains. It removes work rather than overlapping it, needs no new code, and does not touch the accuracy
  posture.
- **`KAOLA_RUN_CHAINS_TIMEOUT_MS` (D-512-01, 900s) remains the correct operational guard** for a
  genuinely-slow-but-passing chain. It is not replaced or supplemented by parallelism.
- **D-526-01's C1 "SAFE but FUTILE" is now refined, not overturned:** on the broader four-chain frame C1
  is NOT futile (a real ideal win exists and attribution is clean) — but it is NOT FINALIZABLE without a
  contended ≤4-core benchmark. The futility verdict was correct for the single claude chain; the
  four-chain frame's blocker is host-validity, not futility.
- **No follow-on build run or Case-A re-plan.** The run terminates with this decision record. A
  concurrent-dispatch build would be authored only later, freeze-once, IF the reopen condition is met.

## Alternatives considered

- **Disposition A — SHIP concurrent `run-chains.js`** (`spawn` + bounded N≤4 pool, per-child env/buffer,
  manual `setTimeout`→kill, canonical re-sort, serial fallback, four-edition #307): the affirmative
  ship-it claim. REFUTED as not finalizable here — A-KILL-3 (attribution) and A-KILL-4 (race) individually
  fail to break it, but A-KILL-1/A-KILL-2 (the make-or-break contended-host makespan) cannot be settled
  affirmatively on an 18-core host, and under the inverted burden an un-settleable affirmative is refuted.
- **Disposition B — DOCUMENT not-worth-it now / selective execution as the zero-risk lever (chosen):**
  the default under the inverted burden and the host-validity gate. Zero code, zero race, zero
  propagation cost; selective execution stays available today.

## Reopen condition

Revisit **only** when **both** hold:

1. The attribution half is already discharged for the four-chain frame (A-KILL-3: 0/5000 mismatches via
   the `KNOWN_CHAINS.indexOf` re-sort; consumers set-wise). A benchmark need only **not regress**
   deterministic ordered chain-level first-failure attribution.

2. A benchmark **on a contended ≤4-core host** — a real CI runner, a constrained cloud VM, or a physical
   box with cores disabled (NOT an 18-core dev box) — shows a **median four-chain makespan win decisively
   exceeding 2× the serial-Σ jitter band** (the realistic ~232s light / ~395s heavy bar) **while
   reproducing deterministic ordered chain-level first-failure attribution**.

Absent that contended ≤4-core number, the null (serial four-chain dispatch) stands, selective execution is
the zero-risk makespan lever, and `KAOLA_RUN_CHAINS_TIMEOUT_MS` (D-512-01, 900s) is the operational guard.
