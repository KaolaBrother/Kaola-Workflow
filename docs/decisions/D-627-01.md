# D-627-01. Debloat the six plan-run and six finalize/workflow-next routing surfaces back to the skeleton target; descope runtime-dead-prose fencing to a follow-up

Date: 2026-07-07
Status: Accepted (partial — see Non-goals)
Issue: #627 (primary — plan-run/finalize/workflow-next routing surfaces had re-bloated past the
       skeleton target with card content duplicated inline; fix#1/#3/#4/#5 shipped, fix#2 descoped)
Related: docs/conventions.md / D-445-01 (existing — the ~150-line skeleton target this decision
         restores compliance with); #623 (same bundle, same `commands/kaola-workflow-plan-run.md`
         lane, co-authored in n1 per the shared canonical spec); `docs/plan-run-cards/join-protocol.md`
         and `docs/plan-run-cards/speculative-open.md` (the existing card homes the debloated blocks
         are re-stubbed to point at); `scripts/test-route-reachability.js` and the four
         `validate-*-contracts.js` (the machine-pinned-token guardrail that makes fix#2 unshippable
         as a prose-only change)

## Context

`commands/kaola-workflow-plan-run.md` (and its 5 sibling plan-run command/SKILL surfaces across the
GitHub/GitLab/Gitea editions) had re-bloated to roughly 480 lines against the ~150-line skeleton
target the project already commits to (docs/conventions.md, D-445-01), with card content — the
wait-budget/escalation/writer-kill-safety ladder and the speculative-open paragraph — duplicated
inline instead of living only in their `docs/plan-run-cards/` card. The six finalize surfaces and six
`workflow-next` surfaces carried related but smaller-scope prose defects: an over-long Goal-Attestation
advisory block, a crash-recovery snippet that used `$CLAIM_JS`/`$claim_script` before its first
assignment, and rotted line-number self-cites left over from earlier edits.

Issue #627 scoped five fixes against this: fix#1 (re-stub the ladder and speculative blocks to their
cards), fix#2 (fence the Codex v1/v2 dispatch + `turn_context.effort` block out of the 3 Claude
commands, and the Claude Teammate-Mode block out of the 3 Codex SKILLs — each block is dead prose on
its non-native runtime), fix#3 (compress the Goal-Attestation block), fix#4 (prefix the resolver so
the crash-recovery and workflow-next entry snippets are self-contained), and fix#5 (fix the rotted
self-cites).

## Decision

**Ship fix#1/#3/#4/#5; descope fix#2 to a dedicated follow-up issue rather than force it in as
prose.**

- **fix#1 (debloat).** The wait-budget/escalation/writer-kill-safety ladder is re-stubbed on the 3
  command surfaces to a short pointer at the existing `<!-- CARD: join-protocol -->` marker
  (`docs/plan-run-cards/join-protocol.md`), while preserving every machine-pinned token
  (`wait_budget_minutes`, `reconcile-running-set`, `writerHalt`, `delegation_outcome`). The 3 SKILL
  surfaces are correctly left untouched — they carry the pinned full A-F Join Protocol inline, which
  is the compliant canonical location for that runtime, not a re-bloat target. The speculative-open
  paragraph is re-stubbed on all 6 surfaces behind the existing `<!-- CARD: speculative-open -->`
  marker, preserving `--speculative-consent` and the `speculative_open_policy: auto` default framing.
- **fix#3 (Goal Attestation).** The Goal-Attestation advisory block is compressed to a short resident
  stub on the finalize surfaces; its enum and rationale are moved verbatim to `docs/api.md`.
- **fix#4 (self-containment).** The one-line `$CLAIM_JS`/`$claim_script` resolver is prefixed before
  the finalize crash-recovery snippet and the `workflow-next` entry snippet on all affected surfaces,
  so each is self-contained rather than relying on a later-in-file assignment.
- **fix#5 (editing scars).** Rotted line-number self-cites are replaced with section cites, and a
  split "Raw output goes to:" sentence is reunited with its path block.
- **fix#2 is DESCOPED to a follow-up — not achievable as a prose-only change.** The exact tokens
  carried by the Codex effort-dispatch block and the Teammate-Mode block are machine-pinned on **all
  six** surfaces, not just their native runtime: `scripts/test-route-reachability.js`'s T5b pins the
  Codex effort tokens (`fork_turns: "none"`, `reasoning_effort: dispatch.codex_reasoning_effort`,
  `fresh child-session effort proof`, `codex_effort_override_unavailable`) on the 3 Claude
  **commands** too, and its T14 pins the teammate sentinel (`NAMED teammate` + the one-request-then-wait
  sentence) on the 3 Codex **SKILLs** too — both mirrored across all four `validate-*-contracts.js`.
  Removing either block from its "wrong" runtime, as fix#2 asked, would turn those contract chains RED.
  Honest fencing therefore requires *relocating* the cross-runtime pins themselves (T5b to SKILL-only,
  T14 to command-only) across the route-reachability test and all four contract validators — that is
  cross-edition **script logic**, which this bundle was explicitly scoped to exclude (it is a
  routing-prose bundle, not a script-logic bundle), and which issue #627 itself names as "the deeper
  single-sourcing/generation option tracked as a separate decision." So #627 ships **partial**
  (fix#1/#3/#4/#5); fix#2 is tracked as a dedicated follow-up issue for the pin-relocation work (filed
  from this bundle's own finalize step — see the CHANGELOG entry for its issue number).

## Consequences

- The ~150-line skeleton target (docs/conventions.md/D-445-01) is restored on the 3 plan-run command
  surfaces; the 3 plan-run SKILL surfaces shrink by the speculative-open restub alone (their ladder
  content is pinned resident, not bloat). All content removed from the restubbed blocks remains fully
  present, verbatim, in its card home — verified by the bundle's code-reviewer and adversarial-verifier
  passes diffing routing-relevant content (not line count) against the pre-debloat version.
- `docs/api.md` gains a new Goal Attestation section carrying the enum and rationale moved out of the
  six finalize surfaces.
- The crash-recovery and workflow-next entry snippets are now copy-paste self-contained.
- The cross-runtime "dead" prose (Codex effort-dispatch tokens on Claude commands; Teammate-Mode tokens
  on Codex SKILLs) remains resident on all six surfaces until the pin-relocation follow-up lands —
  this is the deliberate cost of not touching test/validator logic in a prose-only bundle.
- #627 closes **partial**, not full; the fix#2 follow-up issue carries the remaining work.

## Non-goals

- **No change to `scripts/test-route-reachability.js` or any `validate-*-contracts.js` in this
  decision.** Relocating the T5b/T14 pin scope is explicitly deferred to the fix#2 follow-up issue.
- **No change to card content in `docs/plan-run-cards/join-protocol.md` or
  `docs/plan-run-cards/speculative-open.md`.** Both cards already fully described the restubbed
  content; this decision only removes the inline duplicate.

## Alternatives considered

- **Force fix#2 through as a prose-only edit (remove the blocks from their non-native runtime
  surfaces without touching the pins).** Rejected: it would turn the route-reachability test and all
  four contract validators red on their very next run, since T5b/T14 pin those exact blocks on the
  surfaces fix#2 wanted them removed from. This would trade a documentation nicety for a broken
  standing guardrail — precedence #1 (accuracy) rules it out.
- **Widen this bundle's scope to include the T5b/T14 pin relocation across the route-reachability test
  and four contract validators.** Rejected for this bundle: it is cross-edition script-logic work, a
  different risk class than the routing-prose changes in the rest of this bundle, and the issue itself
  flags it as belonging to its own decision. Keeping it out preserves this debloat's own safety net
  (the guardrail the debloat is verified against is not edited in the same run) and keeps the bundle's
  write lanes exact-file-disjoint and low-risk. Tracked as a dedicated follow-up issue instead.
