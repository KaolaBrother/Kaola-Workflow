# D-693-01 — Derive adversarial gate mode from graph reachability and keep result axes independent

- **Status:** accepted
- **Date:** 2026-07-15
- **Context:** issue #693

## Context

An `adversarial-verifier` used as a read-only investigation could complete with a valid analytical
result, but close treated every instance as a product change gate. A failed/refuted result entered
the repair transaction even though no change producer existed, leaving an empty producer slice and
no writer that `repair-node` could legally reopen. Role-name and prompt-prose checks had also drifted
across evidence seeding, dispatch, close, journal, repair-state, verdict, and Finalization seams.

Execution failure, analytical uncertainty, and gate failure were being overloaded onto one verdict
vocabulary. That made it possible to misclassify missing or stale execution as `indeterminate`, or
to treat a complete investigative refutation as a product-repair request.

## Decision

Use one pure `deriveGateMode(planView, node)` classifier from
`kaola-workflow-adaptive-schema.js` at every lifecycle seam. It returns `null` for any role other
than `adversarial-verifier`. An adversarial node is `change_gate` exactly when:

1. the node can forward-reach the unique sink; and
2. at least one change producer can forward-reach the node.

Otherwise it is `investigation`. The producer set includes code/sensitive producers and the
schema-2 adversarial `certifies` set. This intentionally preserves forward-reachability safety rather
than requiring strict post-dominance: a verifier remains a change gate even if a separate producer
path bypasses it.

Normalize results on three independent axes:

- `execution_status: complete|failed` is harness-derived from terminal execution plus bound
  evidence/context/profile/candidate verification;
- `domain_outcome` is role-domain output (`approved|changes_requested` or
  `refuted|not_refuted|indeterminate`); and
- `gate_effect: pass|fail|none` is harness-derived from role, graph mode, outcome, and admitted
  blockers.

A complete investigation accepts every adversarial domain outcome, derives `gate_effect:none`,
closes, and creates no product-repair journal entry. A change gate passes only on `not_refuted`;
`refuted` and `indeterminate` fail. Missing, malformed, stale, or mismatched execution remains
`execution_status:failed` and routes bounded role retry—it is never analytical `indeterminate`.
Reviewer evidence may not author the two harness-owned fields.

## Consequences

- Required-token seeding, context/dispatch, both close paths, logical-group reduction,
  repair-state projection, verdict checking, and Finalization consume one classifier result.
- Investigation completion cannot deadlock on an empty repair producer slice.
- A model cannot promote an investigation into a gate, demote a change gate, or turn execution
  failure into domain uncertainty.
- Contract-1 frozen plans keep their existing evidence vocabulary; the explicit three-axis receipt
  is part of reviewer contract 2.

## Alternatives considered

- **Treat every adversarial verifier as a gate.** Rejected because investigative probes have no
  product writer and cannot enter a meaningful repair loop.
- **Infer mode from prompt prose or a caller flag.** Rejected because each lifecycle seam could
  drift or be tampered independently of the frozen graph.
- **Require strict post-dominance.** Rejected because it would misclassify a verifier downstream of
  changed code as analytical merely because another route to the sink exists.
