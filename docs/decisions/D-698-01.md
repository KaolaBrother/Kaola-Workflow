# D-698-01 — Bind schema-2 review to candidate context, immutable findings, declared reducers, and a common certifier wall

- **Status:** accepted
- **Date:** 2026-07-15
- **Context:** issue #698

## Context

Legacy verdict evidence did not carry a complete candidate/context/profile identity, finding ids
could depend on model-authored labels or changing proof prose, fan-out reduction was implicit, and
branch-local review could look sufficient even when no one certifier covered the complete producer
frontier. A child plan with no writer could also make inherited unapproved work disappear from the
graph. Repair closure needed durable progress accounting that could distinguish a repair regression
from genuine scope expansion without silently widening the same-lineage loop.

The new contract must coexist with already-frozen schema-1 plans and must not preempt the separate
claim-preserving epoch-activation transaction.

## Decision

Require `plan_schema_version: 2` for newly authored plans and map it exactly to dispatch, context,
receipt, and review-journal contract 2. A verified frozen plan that predates the field remains
contract 1; there is no in-place plan or journal upgrade.

Schema-2 gates declare `gate_claim`, `gate_surface`, `gate_aggregation`, and (for change-gate
adversarial verifiers) a canonical `certifies` producer list. Supported reducers are `sequence`,
`replicated_majority`, and `partitioned_all`. Replicas share claim and surface; partitions share one
claim and have distinct required surfaces. Missing/failed members are incomplete, never votes.
Code/security blockers veto. Adversarial replicated majority requires a strict majority of
`not_refuted`; a refuted tie is `refuted`; partitioned-all is `refuted` on any refuted surface,
otherwise `indeterminate` on any indeterminate surface, otherwise `not_refuted`.

At gate open, persist canonical runtime-neutral context under
`.cache/review-contexts/<context_hash>.json`. It binds plan/behavior/claim/epoch/logical-gate/mode,
claim root, landable candidate, inherited frontier, scope lineage, discovery/closure phase, prior
findings, repair delta, and validation obligations. Runtime-specific resolved profile identity stays
in the dispatch envelope. Before parsing findings, close recomputes contract, context, behavior,
profile, candidate, and graph-mode bindings.

Persist each complete normalized member receipt under
`.cache/review-receipts/<context_hash>/<node-id>.json`. A receipt records harness-derived execution
status and gate effect, role-domain outcome, normalized findings/resolutions, validation vectors,
surface/certifier identity, and raw-evidence digest. Schema-2 change gates write version-exact
transactions to `.cache/review-attempts.json`; complete investigation receipts remain analytical and
create no product-repair attempt.

Use `finding-anchor-v1`. The primary anchor is exactly one of `candidate_range`,
`deleted_base_range`, `tree_entry_change`, `required_absence`, or `evidence_observation`. The harness
validates repository/evidence membership, canonicalizes secondary anchors, recomputes the structured
trigger digest, and assigns the UID from `{scope_lineage_id, primary_anchor}`. Model ids, prose,
proof/resolution digests, and secondary anchors do not define identity. Conflicting immutable records
for one UID refuse.

The first attempt is discovery. Each closure attempt must account for every prior UID, bind new
blockers to the repair delta, bind resolution evidence to the current candidate/repair/vector, strictly
shrink the open frontier, and keep every inherited validation obligation at a comparable pass vector.
Progress updates use a replay-safe idempotency key. A blocker outside the repair delta settles
`review_scope_expanded`; two distinct consecutive non-progress attempts settle
`review_nonconvergent`. Both return `replan_required` and stop the frozen run. The existing
five-consumed-repair ceiling remains.

Add the G4 common-certifier wall. `code_certifier` and conditional `security_certifier` resolve to a
real sequence gate or exact logical group that covers every relevant producer-to-sink route. Group
members must be reachable and the join must require all declared members. `inherited_frontier_digest`
and classes create virtual producers before current roots, including when the child has zero writers.
Finalization recomputes the role-specific current digest and accepts only fresh bound certifier
receipts and comparable validation vectors.

The typed `replan_required` packet is a handoff, not activation. This decision does not choose a
writer or replacement DAG, thaw the frozen plan, perform claim-preserving CAS, write epoch snapshots,
or activate a child epoch; that transaction belongs to issue #699.

## Consequences

- Findings, receipts, reducers, and certifier freshness are deterministic machine contracts even
  though reviewer prose and discovery output remain stochastic.
- Stale candidate/context/profile evidence is rejected before it can influence finding or gate state.
- Investigation liveness is preserved without weakening change gates.
- Repair convergence is bounded, replay-safe, and validation-aware; genuine scope expansion cannot
  be laundered into direct repair.
- Inherited unapproved code/security work cannot disappear through a no-writer child plan.
- Contract-1 plans and journals remain readable and byte-preserving, with no silent migration.

## Alternatives considered

- **Model-authored finding ids or proof-derived ids.** Rejected because wording/proof churn would
  change identity and permit duplicate or disappearing blockers.
- **Implicit reducer choice from fan-out size or role prefix.** Rejected because replicas and
  partitions have different safety semantics.
- **Accept branch-local certifiers.** Rejected because no single fresh receipt would bind the whole
  product frontier.
- **Activate replacement epochs inside close/reducer logic.** Rejected because claim-preserving plan
  swap/CAS is a separate transaction with its own authority and rollback requirements.
