## Context

D-523-01 closed #523 ("genuine suite growth — no safe behavior-preserving reduction") and deferred
the one remaining makespan lever — **race-safe parallelism** — to a dedicated design track, noting
it was "not worth pursuing as a quick fix." That track was filed post-closure as **#526**, carrying
the five hard constraints from D-523-01 §AC#2 as its explicit acceptance gate.

This decision record resolves #526. The run was a **#486 Case-B read-only shaping investigation**
(probe → assume → adversarially falsify → converge). Unlike #523 (analysis-only refutations), this
run executed real timed experiments with measured numbers.

Chain shape recapped for context: claude = 37 `&&`-chained commands ending in
`simulate-workflow-walkthrough.js`; two dominant units (test-adaptive-node ~122s, walkthrough
~160s) account for ≈ 75% of wall-clock. The ~574s reference (D-512-01) is the same structure
under heavier system load.

The burden inversion stated in D-523-01 carries forward: under precedence #1 (accuracy
non-negotiable; a flaky correctness gate is the most expensive outcome), parallelism wins only if
**all five constraints hold AND median makespan win exceeds 2× serial stddev**. Otherwise C4
(serial) stands.

## Question

Is there a race-safe, coverage-preserving, attribution-preserving way to cut chain makespan via
parallelism — worth the risk — or is serial the right permanent posture?

Per precedence #1 the burden is on parallelism: win only if all five constraints hold AND median
win > 2× serial stddev; else C4.

## Decision

**Serial is the right permanent posture (C4). No code change ships.**

The leading candidate C3 (walkthrough-internal N-worker dispatcher) is **refuted as worthwhile**:
its best-case warm win of 4.45s (14.4%) is inside the ~24.5% serial jitter band and below the
>7.8s bar (>2× stddev); it inverts to ~2× slower under oversubscription (a condition a 4-core
target reaches at N=12 = 3× oversubscription); and it mis-attributed first failure 4/5 reps.
Under the inverted burden, the null hypothesis stands. A false ~4.45s "speedup" inside ~24.5%
jitter is a flaky gate, not a win.

## Evidence and measurements

**Host caveat (lead):** All experiments ran on an 18-core host, node v24, `date +%s.%N` timing,
medians over 5–18 reps, no hyperfine. An 18-core host has substantially more headroom than a
4-core CI or laptop target. Every measured win is therefore an **upper bound that does not
transfer to a 4-core host**; conclusions are conservative in favor of parallelism, and still
refuted.

**Exp1 — Serial baseline (5 reps):**
Raw: 50.54 / 39.94 / 44.71 / 41.95 / 43.22s → median **43.22s**, swing 24.5%, stddev ≈ 3.9s.
Decisive bar for any parallelism win: > 2× stddev ≈ **> 7.8s** (corroborates D-523-01's ~28%
jitter finding; load-invariant structural fact, not an absolute second-count).

**Exp2 — Race proxy N=12 (18 reps):**
18/18 zero failures, 216/216 sentinel lines, 0 stderr, 0 race signatures.

**Exp2b — Oversubscribed N=36 / 18 cores (8 reps):**
8/8 clean, 288/288 sentinel — but measured makespan ~85s ≈ **2× slower** than serial 43s. A
4-core target reaches this oversubscription regime at N=12 (3× oversubscription). The win that
appears on the 18-core dev box inverts to a loss on the real target host.

**Exp3b — Fair interleaved warm (5 pairs, serial vs. concurrent):**
Serial median 30.87s vs. concurrent 26.42s → win **4.45s = 14.4%**. Inside the jitter band;
**fails the > 7.8s bar**. Concurrent floor is Amdahl-bounded by the slowest single unit
(~10.3s) + contention — not serial_sum / N.

**Exp4 — First-failure attribution (order [PASS, fail@pos1, fail@pos2], 5 reps):**
Serial correctly reported the pos1 unit all 5 reps. Concurrent "first-to-complete" reported the
**wrong unit 4/5 reps**. Deterministic ordering under concurrency requires buffered per-unit
output + earliest-declared-failure + cancel-in-flight — an unbuilt design cost that does not exist
today.

**Proxy honesty:** The clean race proxy (Exp2/Exp2b, 216/216 + 288/288 sentinel, 0 race
signatures) proves only **process-per-scenario FS isolation** (already structurally evident from
the scenario design). It does NOT exercise in-process hazards: the module-top
`KAOLA_ENABLE_ADAPTIVE=0` env, the `testHarnessSelfCheck` mutate/restore pair, or a real shared
dispatcher. Clean proxy is therefore **not positive proof** of a real dispatcher's in-process
safety.

## Constraint analysis

The five constraints carried from D-523-01 §AC#2, evaluated for leading candidate C3:

1. **Race-safety:** Weakly supported only. Proxy clean (216/216 + 288/288 sentinel, 0 race
   signatures) but proves only process-per-scenario FS isolation — already structurally evident.
   Does NOT exercise in-process hazards (module-top `KAOLA_ENABLE_ADAPTIVE=0`; `testHarnessSelfCheck`
   env mutate/restore) nor a real shared dispatcher. **NOT positive proof. UNMET.**

2. **Shared-tmp serial:** Confirmed hard floor — `runSharedTmpGroup` (:12712–12728) 15 members,
   fixed-order, single shared tmp; guard-throw at :12758. This ~18s lane is indivisible by
   construction regardless of any outer dispatcher. **CONFIRMED hard constraint. MET (unchanged).**

3. **First-failure attribution:** Failed. Fail-fast serial reports the correct unit; naive
   concurrent "first-to-complete" reported the wrong unit **4/5 reps** (Exp4). Correct ordering
   requires buffered per-unit output + earliest-declared-failure + cancel-in-flight — an unbuilt
   design cost. **FAILED.**

4. **Measurement integrity:** Held as method, and is what sank the win. Serial median 43.22s,
   24.5% swing, stddev ≈ 3.9s → bar > 7.8s; warm win 4.45s inside noise; 18-core caveat = upper
   bound. The method held; it returned a refuting verdict. **MET as method; verdict REFUTING.**

5. **#307 propagation:** Worst surface. C3 touches the six-copy walkthrough family (claude root +
   claude plugin + gitlab + gitlab-codex + gitea + gitea-codex); all four chains must stay green.
   C1 (cross-chain) touches only the four-copy `run-chains.js` surface. **WORST COST. UNMET.**

Net: constraints #1 and #3 failed outright; #5 carries maximum propagation cost; only #2 (as a
structural floor) and #4 (as method) cleanly held. A candidate that fails #1 and #3 while not
delivering a decisive win cannot pass the gate.

## Consequences

- **No code change ships.** This is a docs-only record with zero code blast radius; no #307
  obligation attaches to closing #526.
- **KAOLA_RUN_CHAINS_TIMEOUT_MS (D-512-01, 900s) remains the correct operational guard** for the
  genuinely-slow-but-passing chain. It is not replaced or supplemented by parallelism.
- **The D-523-01 → #526 design track is now fully closed.** Spawn-reduction, fixture-sharing (both
  refuted in D-523-01), and race-safe parallelism (refuted here) are all settled. Future readers
  proposing "speed up the test suite" should start at D-523-01 and end here.
- **No follow-on build run or Case-B re-plan.** The run terminates with this decision record.

## Alternatives considered

- **C1 — Cross-chain parallelism:** SAFE but FUTILE. Floor = max(t_chain) = unchanged ~574s claude
  chain; collapsing codex/gitlab/gitea into claude's shadow gains nothing on the bottleneck.
  Four-copy `run-chains.js` propagation surface, no makespan win.
- **C2 — Intra-claude phase parallelism (excluding walkthrough):** REFUTED by inheritance. Floor
  still governed by the indivisible walkthrough; C3 (the stronger sub-case that attacks the
  walkthrough) was refuted — C2 fails a fortiori.
- **C2′ — Constrained intra-claude (validator tail only):** REFUTED as immaterial. Validator tail
  hides under test-adaptive-node ~122s; remaining serial floor ≈ 282s (~75% still serial); saving
  inside the jitter band. Corroborates C4.
- **C3 — Walkthrough-internal N-worker dispatcher (leading candidate):** REFUTED as worthwhile.
  Warm win 4.45s = 14.4% inside jitter (Exp3b); ~2× slower at N=36 (Exp2b); attribution failed
  4/5 (Exp4); proxy clean but proves only FS isolation, not in-process safety; six-copy propagation
  surface.
- **C4 — Serial (status quo, chosen):** CONFIRMED. Zero code, zero race, zero propagation cost.
  Guard stays KAOLA_RUN_CHAINS_TIMEOUT_MS (D-512-01, 900s).

## Reopen condition

Revisit **only** if **both** of the following hold:

1. Either (a) the walkthrough's indivisible units grow materially — the shared-tmp lane or the
   longest single scenario expands enough that the serial floor dominates further — OR (b) a
   hermetic per-worker isolation primitive lands that **provably** eliminates the in-process hazards
   the proxy could not exercise (module-top env, `testHarnessSelfCheck` mutate/restore, real shared
   dispatcher safety).

2. A benchmark **on a contended host** (≤ 4 cores — real CI or laptop, not an 18-core dev box)
   shows a median makespan win **decisively exceeding 2× the serial jitter band** while
   **reproducing deterministic ordered first-failure attribution**.

Absent both conditions, serial is correct and KAOLA_RUN_CHAINS_TIMEOUT_MS (D-512-01, 900s) is the
guard.
