# D-765-01. The dual-accept cutover: retire the legacy full-DAG grammar; the planner authors `plan_form: spine` always

Date: 2026-07-24
Status: Accepted
Issue: #765 (Wave E2 of epic #757 — the final child, deferred to last so dual-accept could not quietly become permanent)
Related: #757 (progressive-elaboration epic), #758–#763 (spine grammar + expansion-point
         lifecycle machinery), #766 (edition port sweep), #767 (Wave E1 — the planner
         spine-authoring lever + one proven full spine run, the precondition for this cutover),
         D-725-01 (adaptive-only consolidation — retired the `fast`/`full` paths),
         #400 (six-surface routing-prose propagation), #307 (four-chain cross-edition parity)

## Context

Epic #757 introduced progressive elaboration: a plan may declare `plan_form: spine` and place
`expansion-point` nodes at milestones whose interior frontier cannot be proven at freeze, deferring
their composition to open time. To ship that incrementally without breaking every existing run, the
plan-validator **dual-accepted** two plan forms:

- `plan_form: dag` (the default; a plan with no `plan_form` field was treated as a dag) — the whole
  task-shaped DAG known at freeze; and
- `plan_form: spine` — with at least one real `expansion-point`.

Dual-accept was always a transition state, not a destination. Two shapes for what is fundamentally
one artifact (a task-shaped graph of role nodes) cost ongoing tax: every fixture, validator branch,
and prose surface had to describe both, and the `spine`-requires-an-expansion-point rule meant a
fully-known plan could not legally be authored as a spine — so the two forms could never converge.

Wave E1 (#767) supplied the missing precondition: it taught the `workflow-planner` to author
`plan_form: spine` and proved one full real spine run end to end. Only then could dual-accept end
without stranding the planner (which, before #767, still emitted full DAGs — absent `plan_form`,
which resolved to `dag`).

## Decision

Retire the legacy full-DAG grammar. `plan_form: spine` becomes the single plan form; a spine is the
task-shaped graph of role nodes, and whether its whole shape is fixed at freeze or a milestone's
interior is composed later is expressed solely by how many `expansion-point` nodes it carries.

1. **Grammar (the plan-validator, ×4 editions — Leg 1).** An explicit `plan_form: dag` AND an
   absent `plan_form` (the old implicit dag) both refuse at the freeze wall with the typed reason
   `plan_form_dag_retired`, ahead of the closed-library check. The refusal is **freeze-only**: a
   verified already-frozen legacy plan still resumes (resume stays dag-tolerant), so no in-flight run
   is bricked. `SPINE-1` is relaxed so a **zero-expansion, all-concrete spine is legal** — that is
   now how a fully-known-at-freeze plan is authored. `SPINE-2..5` are no-ops on a plan with no
   expansion point.

2. **Authoring (the `workflow-planner`, md + 3 forge toml twins — Leg 2).** The planner ALWAYS
   authors `plan_form: spine`. Whole shape known at freeze ⇒ an all-concrete spine with zero
   expansion points (the ordinary case). A milestone whose interior cannot be proven at freeze ⇒ an
   `expansion-point` at that milestone. The planner never authors `dag` (it would refuse
   `plan_form_dag_retired`). `validation_command` + `validation_timeout_minutes` remain REQUIRED for
   any code-producing spine.

3. **Residue sweep (Legs 2 + 3).** With the grammar's second shape gone, the retired-path /
   dual-accept language that survived across the docs, install scripts, agent-facing prompts, the
   twelve generated routing surfaces, and the edition mirrors is rewritten to read as if the current
   version is the only version that ever existed. The live `path_not_installed` typed refusal is
   kept, stated present-tense without its retirement backstory. Structural "DAG" (a spine IS a
   directed acyclic graph of role nodes) is a keep term; only `plan_form: dag` — the retired form
   value — is gone.

## Consequences

- A legacy-shape plan (explicit `dag` or absent `plan_form`) refuses at freeze with
  `plan_form_dag_retired`, naming the spine grammar; a fully-known plan freezes as an all-concrete
  spine. Verified frozen legacy plans still resume.
- The planner, validator, fixtures, and prose all speak one plan form. Token pins that required the
  retired language (the status-line `Workflow path:` pin, the route-reachability re-plan tokens, the
  Step-3 select-project pin, the `fast-summary.md`/`phaseN` needles) were updated in lockstep with
  the residue removals.
- This is a cross-edition change: the four `npm run test:kaola-workflow:{claude,codex,gitlab,gitea}`
  chains, `simulate-workflow-walkthrough.js`, `generate-routing-surfaces.js --check`, and
  `edition-sync.js --check` all gate it.
- Per `CLAUDE.md` § First Principles: axiom 1 (correct first — the grammar retirement fails closed at
  freeze, never mid-run) governs; axiom 3 (spend as little as possible — one plan form, not two)
  motivates ending dual-accept.
